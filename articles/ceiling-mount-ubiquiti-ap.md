# Ceiling-Mounting a Ubiquiti AP: The Right Way to Fix Your WiFi Dead Zones

**Why your router on a shelf isn't cutting it anymore**

---

Most people stick their WiFi router in a closet, on a desk, or behind the TV — then wonder why the signal dies two rooms away. If you're serious about coverage, the ceiling is where your access point belongs.

Here's how to ceiling-mount a Ubiquiti AP the right way, with attic access and PoE — no drywall destruction, no exposed cables, just clean, professional results.

---

## Why Ceiling Mount?

WiFi signals radiate outward and downward. When your AP sits on a desk or shelf:
- Signal gets absorbed by furniture, walls, and people
- Coverage is uneven — strong nearby, weak in adjacent rooms
- Interference from other devices on the same plane

**Ceiling mounting solves this:**
- Clear line of sight to devices below
- Signal spreads evenly across the floor plan
- Less interference, better penetration through walls
- Out of sight, out of mind

---

## What You'll Need

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Ubiquiti AP** (UniFi 6/7 or Ultra) | Your ceiling-mounted workhorse |
| **PoE switch** | Powers the AP over ethernet (no separate power cable) |
| **Cat6 ethernet cable** | Run from switch to AP location |
| **Ceiling mounting bracket** | Ubiquiti's kit or universal mount |
| **Drill + hole saw** | For clean ceiling penetration |
| **Fish tape or pull string** | To run cable through attic |
| **Stud finder** | Locate joists, avoid surprises |

**PoE Budget Check:** A Ubiquiti Ultra pulls about 12W. Make sure your switch has headroom — most managed PoE switches show remaining wattage in their dashboard.

---

## Step-by-Step Installation

### 1. Map Your Dead Zones

Walk your house with a WiFi analyzer app. Note where signal drops below -70 dBm. Mark these spots on a floor plan.

**Ideal AP placement:** Central to dead zones, with clear line of sight to where you need coverage most. Avoid locations above metal ductwork or near microwave ovens.

### 2. Find Your Attic Access

Locate a ceiling spot that's:
- Central to your dead zones
- Near a joist (for mounting stability)
- Within ethernet cable reach of your switch

Use a stud finder to locate joists. You'll mount the AP bracket to the joist, not just drywall.

### 3. Run the Cable

**From your network closet:**
- Run Cat6 up to attic (through wall cavity or existing penetration)
- Use fish tape to pull cable across attic to AP location
- Leave 10+ feet of slack at the AP end

**Pro tip:** Label both ends of the cable before you pull. Future-you will thank present-you.

### 4. Test Before You Commit

Here's the move that separates pros from patch-jobbers:

**Power up the AP in the attic first.**

- Plug into your PoE switch
- Set it on the insulation where you think you'll mount it
- Walk your house and test coverage
- Move it if needed — easy when it's not mounted yet

Only cut the ceiling hole once you've confirmed the location works.

### 5. Mount the AP

- Cut a small hole in ceiling (follow your bracket template)
- Secure mounting bracket to joist
- Connect ethernet, tuck excess cable in attic
- Snap AP into bracket
- Adjust antenna orientation if applicable

**The result:** Clean ceiling install, no visible cables, professional appearance.

## Optimization Tips

**Channel selection:** Use UniFi's auto-optimization, or manually scan and pick the least congested 5GHz channel.

**Power levels:** Don't max transmit power — it causes interference. Start at medium, adjust based on coverage.

**Multiple APs:** If one doesn't cover your whole house, add a second. UniFi makes roaming seamless between APs.
